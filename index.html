Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Timeline

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1958: Joined DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation) as a scientist.
  • 1969: Transferred to ISRO and became the project director for SLV-III.
  • 1980: Developed the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) and successfully launched Rohini satellite.
  • 1990: Awarded Padma Vibhushan for his contribution to science and engineering.
  • 1997: Received Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
  • 1998: Played a key role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.
  • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India and became known as the "People’s President."
  • 2007: Completed presidency and devoted himself to teaching and inspiring youth.
  • 2012: Published the book "Turning Points: A Journey Through Challenges."
  • 2015: Passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong.

About Dr. Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, fondly remembered as the "Missile Man of India" and the "People’s President," was a visionary leader, scientist, and educator. Known for his simplicity, humility, and dedication to the nation, Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's space and defense programs. He authored inspirational books like *Wings of Fire*, *Ignited Minds*, and *India 2020* and served as a mentor to millions of youth.His vision extended beyond technology, emphasizing the importance of self-reliance and scientific innovation in transforming India into a global power. His inspiring leadership in organizations like DRDO and ISRO laid the foundation for India's achievements in space and defense.As the President of India from 2002 to 2007, Dr. Kalam was affectionately called the "People's President" for his approachable and humble demeanor. During his tenure, he championed India's Vision 2020, aiming to make the country economically and technologically advanced. He focused on rural development through initiatives like PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas) and emphasized bridging the urban-rural divide.

Dr. Kalam’s achievements went beyond science. As President, he focused on transforming India into a knowledge superpower and inspired youth with his initiatives and vision. His dream of a developed India led to missions like PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas). His speech, "You have to dream before your dreams can come true," continues to ignite the aspirations of countless individuals.He envisioned a developed and inclusive India through initiatives like PURA (Providing Urban Amenities in Rural Areas), which aimed to improve rural infrastructure. Post-presidency, he dedicated his life to teaching, writing, and mentoring, inspiring millions with his philosophy of dreaming big and working with determination. His books, including Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020, continue to guide and motivate generations.Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong. His death left the nation in mourning, but his legacy as an inspiring scientist, leader, and mentor continues to thrive. He remains a role model for perseverance, humility, and dedication to the betterment of society.Kalam was a passionate advocate for education and youth empowerment. He believed that young minds have the power to transform the nation and often emphasized the importance of dreaming big. His books, such as Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, and India 2020, inspire millions to pursue excellence and contribute to national development. After his presidency, he dedicated himself to teaching, visiting institutions across the country to interact with students and inspire them to innovate and work toward a self-reliant India. Dr. Kalam's timeless message, "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts, and thoughts result in action," continues to resonate with people across the world
